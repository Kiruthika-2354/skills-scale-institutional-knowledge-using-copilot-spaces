# OctoAcme Project Management Process Overview

## Purpose

OctoAcme's project management system is designed to deliver value efficiently through clear, iterative workflows and standardized knowledge sharing. The goal is to centralize process knowledge, enable consistent project execution, and make all team members stakeholders in continuous improvement.

## Key Workflows & Lifecycle

Projects follow five distinct phases: Initiation, Planning, Execution, Release, and Retrospective. Initiation starts with a Project One-pager to validate business needs, identify stakeholders, and define success metrics. Planning breaks approved work into actionable, prioritized backlogs and aligns resources, timelines, and responsibilities. Execution leverages daily standups, weekly syncs, and a structured project board workflow (Backlog → Ready → In Progress → In Review → QA → Done) to drive progress and maintain visibility. Releases require passing tests, documented mitigation plans, and stakeholder announcements, while post-delivery retrospectives convert learnings into actionable improvements.

## Personas, Roles, and Responsibilities

OctoAcme distributes responsibility across distinct personas: 
- **Project Managers** coordinate delivery, minimize risks, and maintain communication.
- **Product Managers** define outcomes, prioritize backlog, and validate success.
- **Developers** build features, uphold quality standards, and contribute to planning and risk mitigation.
- **QA/Testers** validate acceptance criteria and release readiness.
Regular alignment between these roles ensures clarity and shared ownership throughout the project.

## Communication Strategies

Communication is ritualized and structured: daily standups for short-term progress and blockers, weekly PM/Product alignment meetings, bi-weekly delivery team standups, and monthly stakeholder updates. Escalation paths are clearly defined, ensuring blockers and risks are triaged at the appropriate level and resolved promptly. All updates, decisions, and artifacts are documented to maintain transparency and accessibility.

## Quality Assurance & Continuous Improvement

Quality is ensured via layered testing (unit, integration, end-to-end, security scans), well-defined acceptance criteria, and systematic review processes (CI, PR policies, manual QA). Risks are tracked in a Risk Register, reviewed weekly, and addressed proactively. Continuous improvement is baked in—post-release retrospectives capture learnings and produce prioritized actions, which are tracked and reviewed to measure impact and propagate best practices throughout the organization.
